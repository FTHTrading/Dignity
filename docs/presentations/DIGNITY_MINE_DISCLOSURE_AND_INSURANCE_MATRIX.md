## PART I — MINING DISCLOSURE ANALYSIS

### 1.1 The NI 43-101 Situation

**NI 43-101** is a Canadian Securities Administrators standard for disclosure of mineral project information. It is enforced by Canadian provincial securities regulators (primarily the OSC and BCSC) and governs how publicly listed miners in Canada present resource and reserve estimates.

**What NI 43-101 provides:**
- A standardized technical report methodology
- Qualified Person (QP) requirement — a licensed/experienced professional must author and sign the report
- Defined resource/reserve classification tiers: Inferred → Indicated → Measured → Probable Reserve → Proven Reserve
- Prescribed disclosure format for public company material change reporting

**What NI 43-101 does NOT provide in a U.S. institutional context:**
- It is not a U.S. SEC-recognized standard for public company mineral disclosure
- It does not satisfy U.S. Regulation S-K Item 1300 requirements
- A U.S. institutional investor conducting independent diligence on a U.S.-domiciled property owned by a U.S. company will want a U.S.-standard analysis alongside any 43-101 work

---

### 1.2 U.S. Regulation S-K Item 1300 — What It Requires

**SEC Reg S-K 1300** (effective February 2021) governs mining property disclosure for U.S. public company issuers. Key elements:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Qualified Person | Must be independent or with disclosed relationship; credentials defined |
| Technical Report Summary | Required as exhibit to SEC filings for material properties |
| Resource/Reserve Classification | Adopts CIM or CRIRSCO definitions but with specific U.S. requirements |
| Modifying Factors | Must document economic, legal, environmental, title, government factors |
| Preliminary Feasibility Study (PFS) requirement | Required before reserves can be disclosed |
| Initial Assessment disclosure | Required for resources without reserves |
| Price assumptions | Commodity price basis must be disclosed |
| First-time disclosure trigger | Any SEC filing referencing material mining properties |

**Dignity's gap with S-K 1300:**

| S-K 1300 Element | Dignity Status | Action Required |
|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Qualified Person for S-K 1300 purposes | Not confirmed | Identify/retain QP |
| Technical Report Summary | Not prepared | Commission or adapt |
| Commodity price disclosure | Not public | Document assumption basis |
| Title/modifying factor disclosure | Not public | Document title, permits, environmental |
| SEC filing trigger analysis | Not done | Legal analysis of whether filing required |

**Honest framing:**
> NI 43-101 is the best mining disclosure standard Dignity has referenced, and it is widely respected. However, for U.S. institutional credit and disclosure purposes, a memo addressing S-K 1300 applicability and gap-closing steps is required before a U.S.-focused institutional pitch can responsibly claim full mining disclosure compliance.

### 1.3 The Side-by-Side: 43-101 vs. S-K 1300

| Dimension | NI 43-101 | S-K 1300 |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Jurisdiction | Canada (CSA) | United States (SEC) |
| Issuer applicability | TSX/TSXV and other Canadian-listed miners | U.S. public company issuers |
| QP standard | Professional engineer/geoscientist with 5 years relevant experience | Similar, with independent disclosure requirement |
| Resource categories | Inferred, Indicated, Measured | Inferred, Indicated, Measured (CIM-compatible) |
| Reserve categories | Probable, Proven (require PFS or FS) | Probable, Proven (require PFS or FS) |
| Form of disclosure | Technical Report | Technical Report Summary (Exhibit 96) |
| Modifying factors | Required | Required, with explicit checklist |
| Price assumptions | Required | Required with explicit disclosure |
| Cross-recognition | Used by some non-U.S. companies filing with SEC (Form 40-F equivalents) | Required for U.S. domestic issuers |
| Dignity applicability | Referenced | Gap analysis needed |

- UCC liens
- Deeds of trust

**What this means for the institutional diligence table:**

| Element | What's Needed | Dignity Status |

### 2.5 Minimum Viable Insurance Package for Institutional Credibility

An institutional fund will expect at minimum:
1. **D&O** policy in force with coverage summary available
2. **Cyber liability** policy in force with coverage summary
3. **Crime/fidelity** policy in force
4. **Legal opinions** from qualified securities counsel on offering exemption and transfer restrictions

Without these four, a fund's compliance officer will decline to approve the allocation before the investment committee sees the deck.
